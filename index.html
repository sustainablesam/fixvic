Extended to 15 years so like-for-like comparison is meaningful: DST lasts roughly 5 years, PEC's standard reconstruction lasts ~15 years, and grade-A hot-mix asphalt lasts 25‚Äì30 years. The longer timeframe exposes the true cost of cheap decisions.

What is Grade-A Residential-Quality Resurfacing?

DST (Current Plan)

A sprayed bitumen layer with stone chips pressed in. No base repair. No structural improvement. Comparable to painting over a crack. Cost: ~$75K/km. Lifespan: 4‚Äì6 years on a failing base.

SOURCE: PEC Capital Budget INI-9011

PEC "Full Reconstruction"

PEC's own FAQ lists this at $155K/km. Includes base work but uses the County's minimum spec. Can extend road life ~15 years but may not fully address drainage and subgrade issues on a road as deteriorated as Victoria.

SOURCE: PEC Road Rehab FAQ

Grade-A Hot-Mix Asphalt ‚úì

Full-depth reclamation + compacted granular base + 2-layer hot-mix asphalt (HL8 binder + HL3 surface coat). The same standard applied to residential streets in Ontario municipalities. Lasts 25‚Äì30 years. Est. ~$250K/km.

SOURCE: City of Toronto Local Road rates $91/m¬≤ (2025); Eastern Ontario Paving $3‚Äì$7/sq ft residential; PEC Rehab FAQ base rate

Victoria Road-specific patch costs aren't broken out in public budgets, but the pattern is the same: the same craters filled, the same money spent, season after season.
